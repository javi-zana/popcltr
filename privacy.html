1. Who We Are

POP CLTR ("we," "us," or "our") is a Filipino prebiotic soda brand based in Metro Manila, Philippines. We are the Personal Information Controller (PIC) responsible for the personal data collected through this website and in connection with your orders, inquiries, and marketing communications.

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy or the way we handle your personal data, you can reach us at hello@popcltr.com.

Personal Information Controller: POP CLTR  ·  Metro Manila, Philippines  ·  hello@popcltr.com

2. What Data We Collect

We only collect personal data that is necessary for a specific, legitimate purpose. The categories of personal data we collect are described below:

Category Data Points When Collected
Account & Order Data Full name, email address, phone number, delivery address, order history, purchase amounts When you place an order or create an account
Contact Data Full name, email address, message content When you submit our contact form or email us
Marketing Data Email address, preferences, opt-in/opt-out history When you subscribe to our newsletter
Technical Data IP address, browser type and version, device type, operating system, pages visited, time on site, referral source Automatically when you browse our website
Communications Records of correspondence with our team (emails, support tickets) When you communicate with us

We do not collect sensitive personal information (e.g., government ID numbers, health records beyond what you voluntarily share, financial account details). Payment card details are handled entirely by our third-party payment processors and are never stored on our servers.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data only for the purposes set out below:

  • Order Fulfillment: To process your purchase, coordinate delivery, send order confirmations, and handle returns or refunds.
  • Customer Service: To respond to your questions, complaints, or requests in a timely and helpful manner.
  • Marketing Communications: To send you newsletters, product updates, and promotional offers — only where you have given us your explicit consent. You can withdraw consent at any time.
  • Website Analytics: To understand how visitors use our website, identify issues, and improve the user experience. Analytics data is aggregated and anonymized wherever possible.
  • Legal Compliance: To meet our obligations under Philippine law, including tax reporting to the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR), product compliance under FDA regulations, and business registration requirements with the DTI.
  • Fraud Prevention and Security: To detect, investigate, and prevent fraudulent transactions, unauthorized access, and other illegal activities that may harm you or our business.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173), we are required to have a lawful basis for processing your personal data. We rely on the following bases:

  • Performance of a Contract: Processing your order data is necessary to fulfill our contract with you (i.e., to deliver what you purchased).
  • Consent: We rely on your freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent to send marketing emails and to place non-essential cookies on your device. You may withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Legal Obligation: We are required by Philippine law to retain certain records — including transaction records for the BIR, and product safety data for the FDA. We process this data to comply with these obligations.
  • Legitimate Interests: We process Technical Data for fraud detection, site security, and aggregate analytics. We have assessed that these interests do not override your fundamental rights and freedoms.

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for their own marketing purposes. We share your data only with the following categories of trusted partners, and only to the extent necessary:

  • Delivery Partners: J&T Express, LBC Express, and Ninja Van — we share your name, phone number, and delivery address to fulfil your order.
  • Payment Processors: Third-party payment gateways process your card and digital wallet payments securely. We do not receive or store your full card details.
  • Email Service Providers: We use a third-party email platform to send order confirmations, shipping updates, and marketing emails (where consented). These providers act as Personal Information Processors under our instruction.
  • Analytics Providers: We use Google Analytics with IP anonymization enabled. Google may process Technical Data in accordance with its own privacy policy. We do not share identifiable personal data with Google.
  • Legal and Regulatory Authorities: We may disclose personal data to government agencies (e.g., BIR, NPC, law enforcement) when required to do so by law or valid legal process.

We never sell your personal data. Full stop.

All third parties we work with are contractually required to handle your personal data securely and only for the purposes we specify.

6. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to make the site work properly and to understand how it is used. Here is what we use:

  • Essential Cookies (Always On): These are strictly necessary for the website to function — for example, to keep your cart session active or process a secure checkout. You cannot opt out of these without affecting site functionality.
  • Analytics Cookies (Consent Required): We use Google Analytics to track page views, session duration, and traffic sources. These cookies are only placed after you give your consent. You can opt out at any time by visiting tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Managing Cookies: You can control cookies at any time through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to view, block, or delete cookies. Note that blocking essential cookies may prevent parts of the site from working correctly. For instructions specific to your browser, visit its official help documentation.

7. How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by law. Our standard retention periods are:

Data Type Retention Period Reason
Order records (name, address, purchase history) 7 years Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) requirement
Customer service communications 3 years Legal dispute resolution and service improvement

Contact form submissions 2 years Reference for follow-up and dispute resolution

Once the applicable retention period has passed, we will securely delete or anonymize your personal data so that it can no longer be linked back to you.

8. Your Rights Under RA 10173

As a data subject under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173), you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • Right to Be Informed: You have the right to be told what personal data we collect about you, why we collect it, and how it is used — which is the purpose of this Privacy Policy.
  • Right of Access: You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, along with information about how it is being processed.
  • Right to Rectification: If any of the personal data we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to request that we correct it without undue delay.
  • Right to Erasure or Blocking: You have the right to request the deletion or blocking of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, where you have withdrawn consent, or where processing is unlawful — subject to our legal retention obligations.
  • Right to Object: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data on grounds relating to your particular situation, including processing for direct marketing purposes. Where you object to marketing, we will stop processing your data for that purpose immediately.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to receive the personal data you provided to us in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller where technically feasible.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: If you believe that your data privacy rights have been violated, you have the right to file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) of the Philippines. See Section 12 for contact details.
  • Right to Damages: You have the right to be indemnified for any damages sustained due to inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, false, unlawfully obtained, or unauthorized use of your personal data.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at hello@popcltr.com with the subject line "Data Privacy Request." We will respond within 15 business days of receiving your request. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request.

9. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • SSL/TLS Encryption: All data transmitted between your browser and our website is encrypted using industry-standard SSL/TLS protocols.
  • Restricted Access: Personal data is accessible only to authorized personnel who need it to perform their job functions, and they are bound by confidentiality obligations.
  • Secure Storage: Personal data is stored on secure servers with access controls, firewalls, and regular security assessments.
  • Breach Notification: In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the National Privacy Commission within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with RA 10173 and NPC Circular 16-03.

While we take all reasonable steps to protect your personal data, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. We encourage you to keep your account credentials confidential and to contact us immediately if you suspect unauthorized access to your account.

10. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Some of the third-party services we use — including Google Analytics and our email service provider — may process your personal data on servers located outside of the Philippines. When this occurs, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your data in accordance with the standards required by RA 10173.

These safeguards may include standard contractual clauses, data processing agreements, or reliance on the third party's compliance with internationally recognized data protection frameworks. By using our website and services, you acknowledge that your personal data may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries other than the Philippines.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we do, we will revise the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.

For material changes that significantly affect your rights or how we process your data, we will notify you by email (if we have your email address) at least 30 days before the change takes effect. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your data.
