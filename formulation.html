PHASE 02 // GLYCEMIC CONTROL

ACV: Acetic Acid Mechanism.

Unfiltered Apple Cider Vinegar delivers acetic acid (CH₃COOH) — the primary bioactive compound responsible for postprandial glucose attenuation. Acetic acid inhibits disaccharidases in the small intestine, slowing carbohydrate digestion and reducing the rate of glucose entry into the bloodstream. Clinical trials confirm a 19–34% reduction in postprandial glucose area-under-curve (AUC) following vinegar consumption with a carbohydrate meal.

DOI: 10.2337/dc04-1203 [DIABETES CARE / JOHNSTON ET AL.]
PHASE 04 // SYNERGY

Dual-Pathway Gut Support.

Inulin and ACV act through complementary, non-overlapping mechanisms. Inulin operates in the large intestine — fermented by Bifidobacteria into SCFAs that strengthen mucosal integrity. ACV operates in the small intestine — its acetic acid blunts enzymatic starch breakdown upstream. Together, they address both postprandial glucose spikes and long-term gut microbiome composition, covering the full gastrointestinal tract.

PHASE 08 // SAFETY & TOLERABILITY

Regulatory & Safety Profile.

Metabolic safety is the primary pillar of the POP CLTR formulation. Both Chicory Root Inulin and Apple Cider Vinegar are classified as GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe) by the US FDA. The 9g inulin dose sits within the well-tolerated range for prebiotic fibers, and ACV at culinary concentrations has decades of safe consumption data.

GASTROINTESTINAL TOLERABILITY

Chicory Root Inulin ferments progressively in the distal colon, reducing rapid gas production. Gradual dosing introduction (starting at 5g) minimizes any adaptation-phase bloating in sensitive individuals.

FDA GRAS STATUS

Both active ingredients maintain full GRAS status with no reported adverse effects within clinical dosage thresholds — Inulin 5g–15g/day; ACV 1–2 tablespoons/day equivalent.
