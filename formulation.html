NEURO-GASTROENTEROLOGY

The Gut-Brain Axis.

5-HT

The enteric nervous system (ENS) — sometimes called "the second brain" — contains over 500 million neurons lining the gastrointestinal tract, communicating bidirectionally with the central nervous system via the vagus nerve. This Gut-Brain Axis (GBA) is not metaphorical; it is a clinically validated neuroendocrine signaling highway.

Approximately 90% of the body's serotonin (5-HT) is synthesized by enterochromaffin cells in the gut, not the brain. The gut microbiota directly modulates serotonin dynamics — recent 2024 research identified specific strains like Limosilactobacillus mucosae and Ligilactobacillus ruminis that possess the enzymatic machinery (tryptophan hydroxylase) to produce biologically active serotonin de novo.

Reference: Psychobiotics and the Microbiota-Gut-Brain Axis: Advances in Intestinal Serotonin Signaling. Frontiers in Psychology, 2024. | Beneficial Microbes, Vol 16(4), 2025.

NEUROACTIVE MODULATION

The Psychobiotic Paradigm.

PSYCHO
BIOTICS

Psychobiotics — defined as live microorganisms that confer mental health benefits when ingested in adequate amounts — represent the convergence of microbiology and psychiatry. Their mechanisms of action operate across three validated pathways:

01 — NEUROTRANSMITTER MODULATION

Direct influence on serotonin (5-HT), GABA, and dopamine biosynthesis via microbial metabolite production. Specific strains of Lactobacillus rhamnosus have demonstrated GABA receptor upregulation in preclinical models.

02 — HPA AXIS REGULATION

Normalization of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis, reducing cortisol output. This is the biological mechanism behind "stress relief" claims in functional beverages.

03 — INTESTINAL BARRIER INTEGRITY

Strengthening of tight junctions in the intestinal epithelium, preventing translocation of lipopolysaccharides (LPS) — endotoxins that trigger systemic neuroinflammation and are implicated in depression.

Reference: Probiotics as Psychobiotics: A Systematic Review of HPA Modulation. Taylor & Francis, 2024. | MDPI Nutrients, 2024.
MICROBIAL RESILIENCE

Spore-Tech Thermostability.

GBI-30
6086

Traditional probiotics — Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium — are vegetative cells. They exhibit mortality rates exceeding 99.9% in gastric acid (pH 1.5–3.5), pasteurization, and ambient shelf conditions. This is the fundamental failure mode of most "probiotic" beverages on the Philippine market.

POP CLTR utilizes Bacillus coagulans GBI-30, 6086 (trade name: BC30®), a spore-forming strain. The endospore encases the organism in a multilayered protein coat — rendering it dormant and resistant to temperatures up to 100°C, pH extremes, and bile salt exposure. Germination occurs exclusively in the alkaline environment of the small intestine (pH 6.5–7.5), ensuring 100% of the 1 Billion CFU payload reaches its target site.

METABOLIC CASCADE

Short-Chain Fatty Acids.

SCFAs
C₂–C₄

The therapeutic endpoint of probiotic colonization is the production of short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) — acetate (C₂), propionate (C₃), and butyrate (C₄). These microbial metabolites are the currency of the gut-brain axis:

  • Butyrate: The primary energy source for colonocytes, maintaining intestinal barrier integrity and reducing gut permeability ("leaky gut").
  • Propionate: Modulates hepatic gluconeogenesis and lipid metabolism, with evidence for appetite regulation via FFAR3.
  • Acetate: Crosses the blood-brain barrier, directly influencing hypothalamic appetite signaling and neuroinflammatory pathways.

The POP CLTR synbiotic matrix is precisely engineered to maximize butyrate yield through the combination of slow-fermenting prebiotic fibers and BC30 spore germination in the proximal colon.

Reference: Short-Chain Fatty Acids in Gut-Brain Communication. Nature Reviews Gastroenterology & Hepatology, 2023. | Microbial Metabolomics of the Colon. Cell Host & Microbe, 2024.
