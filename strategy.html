01 Scientific Resilience

Technical Moat: POP CLTR is not a beverage; it is a high-performance delivery system. By utilizing 1 Billion CFU Bacillus Coagulans (spore-forming probiotics), we achieve a 95% survival rate through the gastric barrier compared to the 5%-10% seen in traditional yogurt-based cultures.

Scientific resilience is anchored in the "Scientific to Lifestyle" trajectory, ensuring that the 'Clean Girl' aesthetic is backed by PhD-led durability.

Case File: Spore-Forming Synbiotics

The core competitive advantage of POP CLTR lies in its Synbiotic Payload—a combination of probiotics (live beneficial bacteria) and prebiotics (the fiber that feeds them). While international market leaders like Poppi and Olipop have successfully popularized prebiotic fiber sodas, POP CLTR introduces a superior technical moat: 1 billion colony-forming units (CFUs) of Bacillus coagulans GBI-30, 6086.

This specific strain is "spore-forming," meaning it naturally protects itself in a dormant state. This allows the bacteria to survive the harsh, acidic environment of the stomach to reach the intestines where they are most effective. For a startup in the tropical Philippine climate, this tech is vital because it removes the need for expensive refrigerated "cold-chain" logistics, allowing the product to sit on standard store shelves without losing its health benefits.

"The resilience of GBI-30 spores ensures that what is promised on the label is actually delivered to the gut, representing a 100x survival increase over traditional liquid probiotics found in typical grocery store yogurts."
