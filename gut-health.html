Ingredient No. 02

Inulin
Prebiotic Fiber.

Inulin is a naturally occurring fructan fiber found in chicory root, garlic, Jerusalem artichoke, and bananas. It's classified as a prebiotic because it selectively feeds beneficial bacteria in the colon — specifically Bifidobacteria and Lactobacilli — without being digested by the human gut itself.

✓

Feeds Your Good Bacteria — Inulin passes undigested to the colon where it's fermented by beneficial bacteria (Bifidobacteria). This "selective feeding" is the definition of a prebiotic — it only benefits the good guys.

✓

Produces Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs) — As gut bacteria ferment inulin, they produce SCFAs like butyrate, propionate, and acetate. These compounds nourish the gut lining, reduce inflammation, and support immune function.

✓

Supports Calcium Absorption — Clinical studies show inulin improves calcium absorption in the gut, supporting bone density over time — a benefit most fiber sources don't offer.

✓

Low Glycemic Impact — Unlike regular sugars, inulin doesn't spike blood glucose. It contributes a mild natural sweetness (about 10% as sweet as sucrose) while keeping your blood sugar stable.

✓

Shelf-Stable & Heat-Resistant — Unlike live probiotics which require refrigeration and can be killed by heat or acidity, inulin is a stable fiber that works reliably — no cold chain needed.

How Inulin Works In Your Gut
1
You drink POP CLTR. Inulin enters your digestive system intact — it's not absorbed in the stomach or small intestine.
2
It reaches the colon — where 100 trillion bacteria live. Beneficial strains (Bifidobacteria) recognize inulin as food and begin to ferment it.
3
Fermentation produces SCFAs — butyrate, propionate, and acetate. These are the metabolites that nourish your gut lining and regulate immune response.
4
Your good bacteria multiply. Regular inulin intake consistently shifts the gut microbiome toward higher Bifidobacteria populations — documented in multiple clinical trials.
5
Your whole body benefits. A healthier gut microbiome is linked to better mood, stronger immunity, improved metabolism, and reduced systemic inflammation.
Understanding the Difference

PREBIOTICS VS
PROBIOTICS.

Both matter for gut health — but they work very differently. Here's why POP CLTR chose the prebiotic approach, and why it makes sense for an everyday soda.

POP CLTR's Approach

Prebiotics

Prebiotics are non-digestible food compounds — specifically fibers — that selectively feed the beneficial bacteria already living in your gut. Think of them as fertilizer for your inner garden.

  • Stable at room temperature — no refrigeration needed
  • Survive heat, acidity, and shelf life in beverages
  • Selectively feed beneficial bacteria (Bifidobacteria)
  • Work with your existing microbiome
  • Produce SCFAs (butyrate) — key gut health metabolites
  • Consistent efficacy — no viability concerns
Alternative Approach

Probiotics

Probiotics are live bacteria added to foods and supplements. They aim to colonize the gut with beneficial strains — but face several challenges as a soda ingredient.

  • Require refrigeration to stay alive
  • Killed by heat and pasteurization
  • Survive stomach acid inconsistently
  • Difficult to dose accurately in beverages
  • Can be beneficial in the right format (yogurt, supplements)
  • Not ideal for shelf-stable soda formulations
🌱

Think of it like gardening.

Probiotics are like planting seeds — adding new bacteria to the gut. Prebiotics are like fertilizer — feeding and growing the beneficial bacteria already living there. A healthy garden starts with great soil. POP CLTR focuses on making your gut soil exceptional.

How much Apple Cider Vinegar is in each can? +
Each can of POP CLTR contains a meaningful dose of Apple Cider Vinegar — enough to deliver the functional benefits of acetic acid without being overwhelming. We use ACV with the "mother" to preserve the natural enzymes and beneficial compounds.
Will I feel the effects right away? +
Gut health is a long game. While some people notice improved digestion quickly, the real benefits — a stronger gut microbiome, better blood sugar stability, and sustained energy — build up with consistent daily consumption. Think of it like skincare for your gut: consistency is key.

Inulin is safe and well-tolerated by most people. The dose in POP CLTR is calibrated to be effective without being excessive, making it comfortable for daily consumption.
How is this different from other "healthy" sodas? +
Most "healthy" sodas reduce sugar or add vitamins — but they don't actually change your gut health. POP CLTR uses two clinically recognized functional ingredients (ACV and Inulin) that have documented effects on gut microbiome composition, blood sugar regulation, and digestion. Prebiotics like inulin are stable fibers that survive the production process and work reliably every time you crack a can. For a soda, the prebiotic approach is simply smarter science.

Yes — POP CLTR is designed as an everyday drink. Daily consumption is how you get the most out of the prebiotic fiber and ACV benefits. One can a day is a great starting point for most people.
